A LOOMING REALITY

Submerged Magazine is an annual publication exploring the submerged lands that will be the consequence of the climate change issue.
Each topic will be analyzed through the eye of science and art, addressing the environmental issue as never before.

Vol. 0: The water issue

2020, along with 2016, was the warmest year since the 1800s. The average global temperature increased by 1.02°C, which is very close to the critical increase threshold of 1.5°C. The temperature is rising. And rising again. While Trump was saying it was too cold in New York and that global warming would be a good thing for the city, architects and engineers were moving to figure out how to save the Big Apple. Yes, because the glaciers are melting. We don’t need to remind you that. But the more they melt, the closer the water gets to us: eroding our coastlines, flooding our cities, wetting our homes.

And not even the great New York City will survive rising sea levels.
Nor will Venice, Bangkok, London, Amsterdam, Miami, Paris, and the rest of the endless list. Our cities will be partially or totally submerged. But what will man do? Will he abandon them? Or will he find a way to live underwater?
